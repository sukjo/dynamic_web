Kuzumochi

Kuzumochi is a type of mochi cake made from kuzu powder. It is typically served cold, making it an ideal summertime dessert.

Ingredients

  • 100 g (3 1/2 oz.) kuzu powder
  • 50 g (1 3/4 oz.) white sugar
  • 500 ml (2 1/2 cups + 2 tbs). water

Instructions

  1. Mix all ingredients together in a small pan.
  2. Heat over medium-low heat, sitrring consistently. As it heats up, the mixture will start clumping.
  3. Keep stirring vigorously as the mixture gets thicker.
  4. When the entire mixture is translucent and you can lift it off the bottom of the pan, it's done cooking.
  5. Pour the thickened mixture into a square container and smooth out the top as well as you can with a wet spatula. The mixture will get cloudier as it cools. Let it cool down to room temperature, then refrigerate for at least an hour, or until completely cold.
  6. To serve, take the block out of the container and cut it into cubes. Kuzumochi is typically serves with molasses or black sugar syrup and toasted, ground soybean powder. You can also add other toppings to your liking, such as fruit preserves, fresh fruit with honey or syrup, or even condensed milk.
